Propagation: Seed, stem.

Conditions: Shade to sun. Greek prefers full sun.

Roots & Tubers

Solanum spp. (Potato) | Perennial

Edible: Tuber.

Propagation: Tuber, seed.

Conditions: Part shade to sun.

Hardiness: Z7 or likely lower.

Wildlife: Deer sensitive.

Useful Varieties: Solanum Curtilobum and Solanum Ajanhuiri have good frost-resistance and dormancy.

Notes: Establishing a perennial potato patch poses two main problems: disease pressure builds up over the years and tubers may not survive a winter dormancy when left in the ground. Wild potatoes do not suffer from these problems because they are often resistant to both disease and frost, but many wild potatoes are either not safe to eat and/or difficult to harvest. Wild potatoes are also able to spread multiple feet each year via tubers attached to long stolons, allowing them to move around as they grow. Domestication has made potaotes more stationary and more edible but has also weakened their resistances. Finding domesticated species that maintain some of their wild genetics while still being easy to harvest and edible seems to be the best way to incorporate perennial potato patches into forest gardens. Allowing the tubers to move around the garden will also likely significantly improve disease resistance, as they won't be stuck in the same patch of soil year after year. Some species form tubers during short days in fall and some are day-neutral. Short day potatoes are less productive and have a limited harvest window, but for those same reasons may also be less prone to wildlife interference.


Oxalis Tuberosa (Oca) | Perennial

Edible: Tuber, leaf, stem.

Propagation: Tuber, stem.

Conditions: Part shade to sun. Shelter from heat.

Hardiness: Z7 or likely lower.

Wildlife: Deer resistant.

Useful Varieties: Some tuberize a week earlier than others.

Notes: Tubers form during short days in late fall. Tubers may be overwintered in ground. Potential to move around garden. Stems are tart and sour but edible, may be useful as perennial greens with only occasional tuber harvest.

Hardiness: Z7 or likely lower.

Wildlife: Deer sensitive.

Leaves can be used like spinach, may be useful as perennial greens with only occasional tuber harvest. Tubers are hardier than oca and should survive winter easily.


Codonopsis spp. (Dang Shen) | Perennial

Edible: Root.

Propagation: Root, seed.

Conditions: Shade to sun. Protect early growth from heat and frost.

Hardiness: Z4 or Z7

Wildlife: Deer resistant?

Useful Varieties: Codonopsis Pilosula, chinese medicinal vine (Z7). Codonopsis Lanceolata, korean culinary vine (Z7). Codonopsis Bulleyana and Meleagris, hardy upright plant (Z4). Codonopsis Clematidea and Cardiophylla, hardy vine (Z4).

Notes: Some climbing and some upright, suitable for various environments. Tubers grow larger every year, often harvested at 2-3 years old.

Helianthus tuberosus (Sunchoke) | Perennial

Edible: Tuber.

Propagation: Tuber, seed.

Conditions: Shade to sun. Protect early growth from heat and frost.

Hardiness: Z4 or likely lower.

Wildlife: Deer resistant.

Useful Varieties: Helianthus strumosus is grown similarly and has edible roots as well.

Notes: Vigorous grower, great for poor soils. Best planted in a spot where it cannot escape and where management is easy. Roots contain large amounts of inulin. Keeping roots in ground during winter will help reduce this, as will certain methods of preparation. Great for pickling.


Dioscorea Polystachya (Chinese Yam) | Perennial

Edible: Tuber, aerial tuber.

Propagation: Tuber, aerial tuber.

Conditions: Part shade to sun.

Hardiness: Z4

Wildlife: Deer sensitive.

Useful Varieties: Japanese variety also edible, grows similarly.

Notes: Aerial tubers can spread and propagate themselves easily, best planted in a spot where it cannot escape and where management is easy. Roots are fragile, should be grown in bag or tube that can be easily dug up without disturbing root. Root takes about 2-3 years to harvest, aerial tubers are good crop until then. If growing for aerial tubers, best to set aside some roots to leave in ground indefinitely, as older roots will produce more and larger aerial tubers. Needs a large trellis to climb on.

Onions

Allium Cepa Proliferum (Walking Onion) | Perennial

Edible: Bulb, leaf, bulbet.

Propagation: Bulb, bulbet.

Conditions: Part shade to sun.

Hardiness: Z4 or likely lower.

Wildlife: Deer resistant.

Useful Varieties: Varieties differ in number and size of bulbets, size of bulbets. Some varieties have sharper tasting leaves than others.

Notes: Easy to propagate, simply toss bulbets around garden. Varieties with larger bulbets may be preferable if using them directly as food.


Allium Cepa Aggregatum (Potato Onion) | Perennial

Edible: Bulb, leaf.

Propagation: Bulb, seed.

Conditions: Part shade to sun.

Hardiness: Z4 or likely lower.

Wildlife: Deer resistant.

Useful Varieties: Few varieties available, differ slightly in size. Green mountain is a good variety to start with. Same cultivar group as common shallots.

Notes: Slightly different properties than common shallots - can grow larger and store longer, better suited for perennial patches. Most will not set seed and propagation by bulb is easiest. Will produce smaller bulbs if left in ground, and may become prone to disease. Harvest and propagate any afflicted plants.


Allium Fistulosum (Bunching Onion) | Perennial

Edible: Bulb, leaf.

Propagation: Bulb, seed.

Conditions: Part shade to sun.

Hardiness: Z5 or likely lower.

Wildlife: Deer resistant.

Useful Varieties: Varieties differ in color, taste and size. Some more cold hardy than others.

Notes: Useful as another form of scallion, will multiple easily and patches can be divided for propagation. Best to find varieties that are suitably cold hardy for your climate in order to establish a perennial patch.


Allium Sativum (Garlic) | Perennial

Vegetables

Brassica Oleracea (Kale) | Perennial

Edible: Leaf, inflorescence.

Propagation: Stem, seed.

Conditions: Part shade to sun. Will grow in shade but gets leggy.

Hardiness: Z6 or likely lower.

Wildlife: Deer resistant. Bunnies nibble on leaves, but typically don't damage more than a few. Best to share with them.

Useful Varieties: Perennial grex provides good variety, option to select for desirable traits.

Notes: Thicker leafed plants less sensitive to insects. Susceptible to cabbage worm. Should be spaced apart to prevent crowding (plants can spread 3 feet in diameter) and limit insect spread. Should be interplanted with trap crops and pollinator plants to minimize damage.


Colocasia Esculenta (Taro) | Perennial

Edible: Corm, stem, leaf.

Propagation: Corm.

Conditions: Deep shade to part shade. Wet soil.

Hardiness: Z7 or likely lower.

Wildlife: Deer resistant.

Useful Varieties: Many varieties, often confused with elephant's ear, only some are used for culinary purposes. Some more cold-hardy than others.

Notes: Stems provide a perennial alternative to celery, although much spongier after cooking. All part of plant contain calcium oxalate, so must be cooked thoroughly to be safe to eat. Preparation varies, best to do research beforehand. Useful for wet and shady areas.


Hosta spp. (Hosta) | Perennial

Edible: Shoot.

Propagation: Rhizome.

Conditions: Deep shade to sun.

Hardiness: Z4

Wildlife: Deer sensitive.

Useful Varieties: Many varieties, some used specifically for vegetables. Probably best to get those if not interested in experimenting.

Notes: Young shoots make unique vegetables, cut once and then allow plant to grow. Tolerant of most conditions. Useful for deep shade where edible options are limited.


Petasites Japonicus (Japanese Butterbur) | Perennial

Edible: Stem, flower bud.

Propagation: Rhizome.

Conditions: Deep shade to part shade.

Hardiness: Z5

Wildlife: Deer resistant.

Useful Varieties: Petasites japonicus var. giganteus is a larger variety, growing taller with wider leaves. Affect on edible yield is uncertain. Related to Petasites frigidus, arctic butterbur, which has edible properties as well.

Notes: Plant has toxic alkaloids, eat in moderation and always prepare according to traditional methods. Can be an aggressive spreader once established, plant where it will not escape and can be easily managed. Useful for wet and shady areas.

Grains, Seeds & Cover Crop

Sorghum Bicolor (Sorghum) | Annual

Edible: Seed.

Propagation: Seed.

Conditions: Sun.

Hardiness: Z6 or likely lower.

Wildlife: Deer resistant. Birds enjoy seeds?

Useful Varieties: Varieties differ in days to maturity, faster-maturing varieties more suitable for temperate climates.

Notes: Easy to grow in poor and dry soil. One of the easiest grain to process for backyard gardeners. Leaf structure creates little shade and makes it a good plant for vines to climb on. Stalks can be left in ground after plant dies - potential use as trellis or barrier in the next season.


Amaranthus spp. (Amaranth) | Annual

Edible: Leaf, seed.

Propagation: Seed.

Conditions: Sun.

Hardiness: Z3

Wildlife: Deer sensitive. A favorite of deer. Birds enjoy seeds.

Useful Varieties: Some varieties more suitable for seed production, others better for edible leaves.

Notes: Easy to grow in poor and dry soil. Reseeds itself generously, even without reaching maturity. Best species in amaranth family for grain production. Seed is easy to harvest and clean, but are small compared to other grains.


Chenopodium Nuttalliae (Huauzontle) | Annual

Edible: Leaf, inflorescence, seed.

Propagation: Seed.

Conditions: Sun.

Hardiness: Z5 or likely lower.

Wildlife: Deer sensitive. A favorite of deer. Birds enjoy seeds.

Useful Varieties: none.

Notes: Easy to grow in poor and dry soil. Reseeds itself generously. If growing for seed or leaf, other species in amaranth family may be more productive. Unlike other related species, though, this plant is often used for its edible inflorescence. As far as edible inflorescence production goes, this plant has amazing potential both for it's ease of growth and the quantity a single patch can produce. If I could only grow one plant in the amaranth family, this would be it.


Atriplex Hortensis (Orach) | Annual

Edible: Leaf, seed.

Propagation: Seed.

Conditions: Sun.

Hardiness: Z5 or likely lower.

Wildlife: Deer sensitive. A favorite of deer. Birds enjoy seeds.

Useful Varieties: Varieties come in a rainbow of colors.

Notes: Easy to grow in poor and dry soil. Reseeds itself generously. Good species for leaf production, easy spinach subsitute.


Celosia Argentea (Plumed Cockscomb) | Annual

Edible: Leaf, seed.

Propagation: Seed.

Conditions: Sun.

Hardiness: Z5 or likely lower.

Wildlife: Deer sensitive.

Useful Varieties: A few different varieties, some growing taller than others. Celosia trigyna is said to have edible leaves as well but is not worth growing.

Notes: Easy to grow in poor and dry soil. Reseeds itself generously. All reports indicate leaves must be cooked. Gorgeous flowers, some reports of inflorescence being eaten but the specifics of that may depend on the variety.


Helianthus Annuus (Sunflower) | Annual

Edible: Flower, seed.

Propagation: Seed.

Conditions: Sun.

Hardiness: Z6 or likely lower.

Wildlife: Deer resistant. Birds enjoy seeds.

Useful Varieties: Some have multiple smaller flowers and some have a single large flower.

Notes: Easy to grow in poor and dry soil. Large edible seeds. The young, unopened flower head can be prepared and eaten as a vegetable similar to artichoke/cardoon heart. Great potential as vegetable, especially for multi-headed varieties.
